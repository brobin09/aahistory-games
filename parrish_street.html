WHO YOU ARE

You sit on the boards of Black Durham's institutions across seventy-two years, from the founding of N.C. Mutual in 1898 to the closing of the books in 1970. The street below is yours to walk — Parrish Street downtown, the railroad, and Hayti beyond it.

HOW A SEASON PLAYS

History moves first — an event arrives, sometimes with a decision, sometimes with a document to read like a historian. Then the board acts — walk to any site marked with a brass seal and choose one initiative. What you build appears on the street. What you lose disappears from it.

WHAT THE NUMBERS MEAN

CAPITAL — money to build with. Institutions earn it every season.
TRUST — the community's faith in the Mile. It drains a little every season; watch the sidewalks fill and empty with it.
DEPENDENCE — how much you owe white Durham's goodwill. Cheap now, expensive later.
ARCHIVE — the historical record you build by reading documents well.
THE GOAL — reach 1970 with the highest LEGACY (watch it live on the brass plaque): institutions standing, trust held, archive full, dependence low. Build early. The final act is coming, and it cannot be stopped — only answered.

AND THE MILE CAN FALL — if TRUST ever reaches zero at a season's close, premiums lapse, deposits walk, and the ledger closes early. Money recovers. Faith, once gone, does not.
