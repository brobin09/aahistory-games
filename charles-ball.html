✦ A TRUE ESCAPE · c. 1809–1810 ✦

CHARLES
BALL

North by the Stars. Sold away from his wife and children, Charles Ball slipped out of the Georgia cotton lands and walked home to Maryland — hundreds of miles, moving only in darkness, hiding through every daylight hour, steered by one fixed point in the sky. He did not do it alone. Nobody did.
MOVE BY NIGHT · HIDE BY DAY · EAT WHEN YOU CAN · THE STAR IS THE ONLY MAP
The sky strobes — a short night to run, then a brief, killing daylight. When DAY breaks you must already be hidden — inside a shelter, or under dense canopy — or a hand closes on your collar. Shelters and cover are scattered and hard to find, so mark them as you go.

Drawn from Ball's narrative, published 1836 and reissued as Fifty Years in Chains. The people who help you on this road — enslaved, free, and a few whites — stand for the thousands the record names and the many more it never could. What they risk in these pages, people truly risked.
